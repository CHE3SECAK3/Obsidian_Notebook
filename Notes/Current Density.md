# Current Density
- *The amount of current per area of wire*

- Symbol : $J$

- Units : $\frac{A}{m^{2}}$ where:
	- $A$ : Amps
	- $m^{2}$ : Cross-sectional area of wire

> [!note] Current Density and Electron Current
> $$\begin{align*}
J &= \frac{I}{\text{A}} \\\\
&&I &= ei_{e} \\\\
&&i_{e} &= n_{e}Av_{d} \\\\
J &= e n_{e} v_{d}
\end{align*}$$
> - where:
> 	- $I$ : current ($\text{Amps}$)
> 	- $A$ : cross-sectional area of wire ($m^{2}$)
> 	- $i_{e}$ : Electron current
